In celebration of October’s designation as National Cookbook Month we are featuring  Favorite Recipes of Moscow Ladies published in 1896. The book is part of the Day-Northwest Collection found in the University of Idaho Library Special Collections and Archives. For Fall, we thought a couple of nice soup recipes would be just the thing. 

The cookbook contains recipes from members of the Ladies Aid Society of the Methodist Episcopal Church in Moscow, Idaho. The front cover declared it was a cook book “containing the choicest recipes for economical and practical housekeeping. Another cookbook found in the Day-Northwest Collection is Choice Recipes published in 1931 by the Hospitality Committee of the Presbyterian Ladies Aid of the Presbyterian Church in Moscow. This cookbook also offers some warm, filling soup recipes.

The Day-Northwest Collection consists of published materials for the study of Idaho and the Pacific Northwest. The materials included relate to persons, places, events, and things in and of the geographical area now known as the State of Idaho, together with materials that deal with Idaho's place in the region and the nation. These books have the designation DAY-NW in the bibliographic system.
