Welcome back to another week of Women’s Recreation Wednesday, our series covering the Women’s Recreation Association here at the University of Idaho.

This week’s photos feature women skiing! The first ever recorded ski jumping event was performed by a Norwegian woman named Ingrid Olavsdottir Vestby who is said to have “soared 20 feet” in Trysil, Norway in 1862.[1] However, it wasn’t until 1990s when women were “first allowed to fully participate in international jumping competitions. In 2006 regarding the Olympics, the International Ski Federation (FIS) and the International Olympic Committee “ruled that there was not enough technical merit among women ski jumpers to allow them on the jumps” but quickly clarified that “the decision had nothing to do with gender.”[2] “Women’s jumping were not on the schedule at the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, but they [were] in 2014 at Sochi, Russia.”[3]
