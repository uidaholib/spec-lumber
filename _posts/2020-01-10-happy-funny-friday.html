Humor Mottos

  • A person without a sense of humor is like a wagon without springs - jolted by every pebble in the road. (Henry Ward Beecher)
  • If we could not laugh, we would all go insane. (Jimmy Buffet)
  • The best way to cheer yourself is to try to cheer somebody else up. (Mark Twain)
  • It is not so important to be serious as to be serious about the important things. The monkey wears a serious expression which would do credit to any great scholar. But the monkey is serious because Â he itches! (Laroff's Credo)
  • The trouble with political promises is that they go in one year and out the rest. (S.H. Simmons)
  • Humor is laughing at what you haven't got, when you ought to have it. (Langston Hughes)
  • I've developed a new philosophy … I only dread one day at a time. (Charlie Brown)
  • Just because you're miserable, doesn't mean you can't enjoy your life. (Annette Goodheart)
  • Humor is the great thing, the saving thing, after all. The minute it crops up all our hardnesses yield, all our irritations and resentments flit away and a sunny spirit takes their place. (Mark Twain)
  • I do not want it said of our generation, as T.S. Eliot wrote, “These were decent people, their only monument the asphalt road, and a thousand lost golf balls.” We can do better than that. (John F. Kennedy)
  • At the height of laughter the universe is flung into a kaleidoscope of possibilities. (Jean Houston)
  • If I can make you laugh with me, we will forget our differences, and long after the laughter has died down we will remember our likeness. (Russ Fischer)
